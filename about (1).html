Behind the Camera

The Story

On Set Wilmington was built on a simple belief: a city with forty years of film history deserves more than a map and a self-guided walk. It deserves the real thing — led by the people who lived it.

Wilmington has spent four decades as one of America's busiest film towns — and yet, for years, the only way to experience that legacy was to wander downtown with a printout and guess which corner was in which movie. We thought the people who love film deserved better.

So we built On Set Wilmington — a company dedicated to putting you on the real locations, with real stories, told by people who've actually worked in the industry. Not a sightseeing bus. Not a trivia quiz. An insider's pass to Hollywood East.

"We don't point at buildings from across the street. We walk you onto set."

On Set Wilmington is powered by ShoreList, the local marketplace built to showcase the best of Wilmington and the beaches. That partnership means every experience is backed by a trusted booking platform — and that supporting us means supporting the local film and tourism community.

Your Guide

Three Decades On Set

Our co-founder Ron has spent roughly thirty years working in film and television production — the kind of career that means he doesn't read these stories off a card. He's been on the sets, knows the locations the way only a working crew member can, and remembers what the city looked like before the cameras showed up.

That's the difference. When you take an On Set Wilmington experience, you're not getting a script — you're getting someone who was there, telling you what really happened between "action" and "cut."
